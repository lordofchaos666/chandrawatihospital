üìè Growth & Milestones

  • Weight Check: Newborns lose weight in the first week but regain it by day 10.
  • Red Flags: No neck holding by 4 months, not sitting by 9 months, no words by 15 months.
  • Height: Height usually doubles by 4 years of age.
  • Teething: First tooth usually appears between 6-8 months.
×

üíâ Vaccination Guide

  • Painless vs Painful: Painless (Acellular) vaccines cause significantly less fever and swelling than whole-cell vaccines.
  • Post-Vaccine Care: Do not massage the injection site. Use a cool cloth for swelling.
  • Missed Dose? Don't panic. Consult us to reschedule. It is never too late to catch up.
  • Flu Shot: Recommended annually for children under 5 years.
×

ü•¶ Nutrition & Diet

  • Brain Foods: Eggs, Fish, Nuts (powdered for toddlers), and Yogurt.
  • Iron: Essential for IQ. Found in Green Leafy Veggies, Jaggery, and Dates.
  • The No-No List: No Salt, Sugar, or Honey/Cow Milk before 1 year of age.
  • Picky Eaters: Don't force feed. Offer small, colorful portions frequently.
×

üì± Screen Time & Eyes

  • 0-2 Years: Zero screen time. Brain development relies on real-world interaction.
  • 2-5 Years: Max 1 hour/day of educational content.
  • 20-20-20 Rule: Every 20 mins, look 20 feet away for 20 seconds to reduce eye strain.
  • Blue Light: Stops sleep hormone (Melatonin). No screens 1 hour before bed.
×

ü§± Exclusive Breastfeeding

  • No Water Needed: Breast milk is 88% water. It hydrates the baby perfectly even in summer.
  • On Demand: Feed whenever the baby shows hunger cues (licking lips, sucking fingers).
  • Fore-milk vs Hind-milk: Fore-milk quenches thirst; Hind-milk (thicker) provides fat for weight gain. Empty one breast fully before switching.
  • Mother's Diet: Eat healthy. No strict restrictions unless the baby shows specific allergies.
×

ü•£ Starting Solids (6+ Months)

  • Day 1-3: Start with single-grain cereal (Rice/Ragi) or mashed apple.
  • Texture: Start with smooth puree, move to mashed/lumpy by 8 months.
  • Food Groups: Introduce vegetables early so they don't develop a sweet tooth.
  • Allergy Check: Introduce new foods one at a time and wait 3 days to check for rashes.
